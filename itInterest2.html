IT Interest


My first Job was working as a part time cleaner at a large printing factory. On the 3rd level, in the executive area, near a framed photograph of Rupert Murdoch hanging on the wall, there was a big, locked, glass panelled, air conditioned room containing a series of large blue fridge style machines. I watched the workers occasionally, it was staffed 24×7, but the staff sat outside the room. There was a sticky pad on the floor just outside the doorway. No one else, other than the three shift workers were allowed to go in the room. As I was polishing the floor outside, I covertly checked out the exercise book the staff kept updating – it seemed to be a log of what went on. Sometimes the staff got up from their desk, went into the big room, looked at all the machines and changed tapes. The staff did not have a monitor at their desk. After I garnered the courage to ask what the blue machines did, I was surprised to find out it was nothing to do with actual printing, but apparently they performed “other important tasks”. I had to find out a bit more about this..


Although I found out later, the role of the computers were “just” running father & son batch account and ordering updates throughout the night, I was intrigued by it all. Fast forward a couple of years, I became a Computer Operator in the public service, on shift work, looking at blue (sometimes orange which apparently were a rip off of the blue) machines, changing tapes and printing great heavy paper stacks every night.


While I did not pursue a specific IT degree, I remained heavily engaged in all things computers and self taught to program. I am most interested in Raspberry Pi’s and Arduino’s now, trying to combine my interest with Amateur Radio. I can communicate around the world I digital (test) mode without using any other infrastructure other than my HF radio and a Raspberry Pi.


Why RMIT

It was more the Open University study options that attracted me rather than RMIT specifically. All the things, for whatever reason I didn’t do when I was younger, I was now able to study eg chemistry, maths, programming etc on a piecemeal basis. Why not bundle the subjects, and try to attain an IT degree, which is where I am now? At RMIT.


Expectations

As mentioned, the number one goal is to attain the degree. But having said that, I’m not sure why it seems important to me.. My career is unlikely to change. I’m not young, far from it. But having focused on the technical subjects so far, I think it’s time to go back and do the degree the way it is meant to be according to it’s structure, ie filling in 1st, 2nd and 3rd year requirements, in order. I also feel that since I’m a paying student through coming through the OUA format, that I’m not taking up a place a younger person should have as a career launcher.


One thing that has changed over the years is a new emphasis on working in teams, so that will be interesting. This aspect is emphasised in this Introduction to IT subject, so I’m looking forward to that while being slightly apprehensive at the same time. I’m also looking forward to the non techie material such as ethics and the social aspects of computing in light of machines become more and more capable. 26 Billion transistors now on a graphics board – just can’t get my head around that!Learning about the concepts of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning will be timely in view of the mainstream publicity these aspects are getting and the workforce is likely to be affected.
